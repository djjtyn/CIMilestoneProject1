Compression/Limiter

Both the compressor and limiter tools affect the dynamics of the audio in a recording. Audio dynamics deal with the volumes of the audio. Both of these tools work by making the quieter parts of a recording louder and decreasing the louder parts of the recording.For a further explanation of dynamics, see the video below:

Compression

Compression lessens the dynamic range between the loudest and quietest parts of an audio signal. The loudest parts of the signal are attenuated and the quieter parts are boosted in volume. Usually the controls on all compressors are the same which are:

Threshold: This is how loud the signal has to be before the compression is applied.

Ratio: This is how much compression is applied. If a compression ratio is set to 5:1, the input for the compressor would have to cross the threshold by 5 decibels for the output level to increase by 1 decibel.

Attack: This is how quickly the compressor starts to work once the signal passes the set threshold.

Release: This is how soon after the signal drops below the threshold that the compressor stops being applied.

Knee: This sets how the compressor reacts to signals after the threshold is passed. A high knee setting would mean that the signal is affected as soon as the threshold is passed, wheras, a low knee setting allows the compression to be applied more gently as the signal goes further past the threshold setting.

Gain: This allows the user to adjust the volume of the affected signal to a higher level after the compression is applied.

Limiter

Limiters are very similar to compressors. They also lessen the dynamic range between the loudest and quietest parts of an audio signal. A limiter completely prevents a signal volume from going over a specified threshold setting. Limiters are more noticeable when applied,wheras a compressor is gradually applied and less obvious the signal is being affected.

The limiter is a good tool for ensuring that any of the signal doesnt distort even at its loudest part.

The biggest difference between using a compressor and a limiter is that the ratio setting on a limiter is set much higher than the ratio setting on a compressor.

The limiter is the last audio tool in the audio processor chain and is used for mastering the audio signal. The limiter is used to increase the overall volume of the audio signal.
